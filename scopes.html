Literature Survey

Diabetes Mellitus (DM) is one of the most common chronic diseases at present, a disease that cannot be cured but can be treated by medications and healthy lifestyles. To prevent fatal complications, treatments need to be provided. Diabetes has different stages, such as prediabetes, type 1 diabetes, type 2 diabetes, etc. , and each stage can be diagnosed by different types of medical laboratory tests. Typically, a sample of blood, urine, or other bodily fluids are taken by medical laboratories for tests to diagnose or monitor diseases, and for diabetes, mostly blood samples are taken into consideration. A few of the blood tests that are taken to diagnose diabetes are, HA1C, FBS, HDL, etc. Patients who are diagnosed with diabetes should track their blood glucose levels regularly. Even though blood sugar monitoring devices exist, the inability to afford one has led patients to visit medical laboratories and doctors regularly. According to doctors, most of the patients lack the knowledge of their diseases, symptoms, and medications. Thus, to get a comprehensive analysis, patients must visit a physician who is a time-consuming process. According to the literature review, it was discovered that few machine learning techniques had been used to screen prediabetes and diabetes. The KNHANES 2010 data (n = 4685) were used for training and internal validation, while data from KNHANES 2011 (n = 4566) were used for external validation. Two models were developed to screen for prediabetes using an artificial neural network (ANN) and support vector machine (SVM). They performed a systematic evaluation of the models using internal and external validat io n. Research Gap

According to the related works based on developing the accuracy of the extracted data from an Optical Character Recognition, it was found that systems have been proposed to build a medical report reader. Tesseract OCR is not capable of identifying some characters. Micro (μ), which is used as a unit of volume, according to previous research, they failed to identify this character. Since medical reports use this unit more often, Tesseract could not identify this character. It does not have the type of the test name in the report. OCR by itself cannot identify the type of the report, and at present, no solution has been provided to handle such a situation. Recent studies have shown that the stress level of the patient is also one of the main factors to consider when diagnosing prediabetes or diabetes . The Scopes of most of the existing studies are limited to implementing a machine learning model which facilitate the general input factors which mentioned above, and there arises a need to study and analyze the procedure of diagnosing/predicting prediabetes/ diabetes with a high percentage of accuracy considering the factors extracted from the medical reports and the stressmarker. Recent studies based on related works on early diagnosis of diabetes, the progression of Body Mass Index(BMI), blood glucose level, and combined with age and gender should be taken as input data to train the prediction model. Currently, there are no systems available to track the data on period and predict the probability of being diagnosed with cardiovascular diseases due to the prevalent conditions of Diabetes, which arises the need for the proposed system. AACE/ACE (American Association Of Clinical Endocrinologists American College 6 Of Endocrinology) Comprehensive Type 2 Diabetes Management Algorithm has not been implemented in related systems at present. According to the literature survey details gathered, most of the existing solutions are developed to work as a medical chatbot. Those interaction systems are not developed, aiming for a specific area. Machine Learning algorithm will be used to find the most suitable patient based on the patient’s details.

Research Problem

As per recent statistics by the International Diabetes Federation (IDF), one in 12 adults has diabetes in Sri Lanka, which is approximately equal to 1.16 million of the population. The anticipated diabetes predominance for the year 2030 is 13.9%. Diabetes is a disease that cannot be cured, whereas prevention and control are the only two ways to treat diabetes. Many people get diagnosed with diabetes due to a lack of knowledge of their symptoms and diseases. When instructions to mainta in diabetes were not given to patients on time, the situation can lead them to increase the risk of developing their disease to the next level. Patients are unable to track the progression of the disease and the progression of the key factors that affect cardiovascular diseases as currently, there is no system capable of achieving this task. To control this disease, medications and regular monitoring are required throughout life. Therefore, the patient should frequently visit a physician who is tiring and time - consuming for the patient as well as for the doctor. To bridge this gap between the doctor and the patient, no practical solution has been implemented yet. Expendit ures for medications are also considerably high. Several mobile applications exist at present to help diabetic patients. For most of the applications, data needs to be entered manually, and then the application keeps track of the medications, food, blood sugar levels, etc. According to the entered values, the system suggests suitable food or medications. Since patients lack the knowledge of medical terms, entering values manually is error-prone and time-consuming. Thus, an application is needed which can diagnose /predict pre-diabetes or diabetes and make suggestions for a healthy lifestyle by scanning and analyzing a medical laboratory report while bridging the gap between the patient and the doctor.

Research Objectives

Main Objective

This research mainly focuses on developing a mobile application that can diagnose diabetes by analyzing a medical report. Condition of the disease will be predicted to patients to control diabetes, efficiently and effectively. This application will track all the previous reports of that patient and other conditions of them to give informa t ion about their current stage. Also, from records, the application will predict the future risks that can be caused to the patient. The application helps the patient to connect and communicate with doctors and get the necessary information as well.

Specific Objectives

1.

Accurately extract the data from a report and assigned to medical terms. As the first step, the patient can scan medical reports from the application. Then Convert the image captured by the user into a quality image so that the Tesseract OCR can easily extract data from the image. For this task, OpenCV and image processing techniques will be used. Utilize a dictionary to improve the accuracy of the extracted data. This can be done via an API call to a spellchecker. Then build a knowledge graph to identify medical terms more efficiently and accurately. Also, maintain a database of extracted data for further analysis.

2.

Implement a machine learning model to diagnose/ predict prediabetes or diabetes and provide recommendations. Using the data extracted from the OCR implement a machine learning model while improving the accuracy of the model with AACE/ACE (American Association Of Clinical Endocrinologists American College Of Endocrinology) Prediabetes Algorithmand Glycemic Control Algorithm. Also and with the ISMA (International Stress Management Association) Stress Questionnaire. The application provides calorie intake suggestions based on the outcomes of the machine learning model and finally provide exercise suggestions based on the machine learning model.

3.

Implement a machine learning model to underatand given inputs and predict if the user has a probablity of cardio-vascular disease. The aplication provide insight on the risk factors and provide guidance for users to adjust to a healthier lifestyle. The application provides historic details of the previous prediction results. The users will be able to obtain a comprehensive understanding of the risk factors and the progression since the last medical check-up.

4.

The application connects the Patient, Doctor, and application through a chat interface. Patients can ask any medical-related questions, especially questions around diabetes, then the application answers those questions. If the question cannot be answered, those questions with the patient’s profile and medical history will be sent to a doctor for accurate and correct answers. Also, the application suggests the best Doctor based on the patient’s conditions. Doctors will be suggested based on patient’s inputs to help with side effects.
